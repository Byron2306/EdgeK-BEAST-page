BEAST Commons Registry

Spaces for work you never want to pay to recompute.

BEAST Commons is not a place to run demos. It is a registry of verified compute-displacement artifacts: manifests, hashes, receipts, verifier bundles, provider-fitness cards, and local-first adoption gates. The cloud publishes hypotheses. Local BEAST decides trust.

What BEAST Commons is measuring

The first Tiny Llama gateway-repair Space is not “faster than baseline.” The optimized route is much slower: roughly 15.9s vs 648ms. That is the wrong comparison. The baseline failed with capability_failure; the optimized route succeeded with verification_passed. This is broken vs working, with latency as the price of a tiny local model plus verifiers instead of blind cloud recomputation.
